==============================================
  HOW TO DEPLOY RESEARCH HUB ONLINE (FREE)
==============================================

WHAT YOU NEED:
  - GitHub account (free) - github.com
  - Render account (free) - render.com
  - Domain name (paid, ~$10/year) - namecheap.com

----------------------------------------------
STEP 1: UPLOAD TO GITHUB
----------------------------------------------
1. Go to github.com and sign in
2. Click "New repository"
3. Name it: research-hub
4. Click "Create repository"
5. Upload ALL files from this folder to the repo
   (drag and drop in the browser)
6. Click "Commit changes"

----------------------------------------------
STEP 2: DEPLOY ON RENDER (FREE HOSTING)
----------------------------------------------
1. Go to render.com and sign in with GitHub
2. Click "New" then "Web Service"
3. Select your "research-hub" repository
4. Fill in settings:
     Name:          research-hub
     Runtime:       Node
     Build Command: (leave empty)
     Start Command: node server.js
5. Click "Create Web Service"
6. Wait 2-3 minutes for deployment
7. Render gives you a FREE link like:
     https://research-hub-xxxx.onrender.com

----------------------------------------------
STEP 3: ADD YOUR CUSTOM DOMAIN (OPTIONAL)
----------------------------------------------
1. Buy a domain from namecheap.com (~$10/year)
   Example: myresearchhub.com
2. In Render dashboard:
   - Go to your service settings
   - Click "Custom Domains"
   - Add your domain name
3. In Namecheap:
   - Go to DNS settings
   - Add CNAME record pointing to Render URL
4. Wait 24 hours for DNS to update
5. Your site will be at: https://myresearchhub.com

----------------------------------------------
PAPER LINKS (SHARING)
----------------------------------------------
Each paper has its own permanent URL you can copy and send to anyone:
  https://yourdomain.com/papers/1
  https://yourdomain.com/papers/2
  ...

Dr. Ibrahim Al-Hassan's paper:
  https://yourdomain.com/papers/361

When someone opens that link they see the paper page, and when they
click "Open PDF" the Columbus University viewer opens (with the
university logo in the browser tab), where they can read or download
the full dissertation.

You can share these links with anyone!
